Welcome to part I-lost-count of Tofu's identity crisis. Today we will ponder, why is Tofu Tofu?

Something I noticed about me is that, I don't like describing myself as "I am something," and I prefer "I like doing something" instead. I am not an artist, but I like drawing. I am not a programmer, but I like coding. Maybe I just hate labeling myself. I am Tofu and I am nothing else. And Tofu likes doing things sometimes. I mean, doesn't this make sense? I draw sometimes, and sometimes I don't draw. What did you expect, to draw 24/7? I'm hesitant to label myself an artist because it makes me feel like I have to draw 24/7 or something.

Another thing is, I feel like I treat myself like a program, and when something is wrong, I look for what exactly caused the problem as if I can just fix a line of bug and get myself running normally again. Sometimes it works out, like that time I had bad sleep for 4 days in a row and literally felt like dying. I fixed that by, y'know, sleeping. But sometimes I can't find a fix, or it can only be fixed through time or effort, like anxieties about class assignments and registration. And I have this delusion that whenever I'm feeling down, I just need sugar; so I drink a bunch of cola and eat a bunch of chocolate. I know people are like, complicated creatures that aren't just "plug stuff in and get results," but it's still hard to accept that.
