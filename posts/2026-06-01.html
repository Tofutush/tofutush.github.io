May Recap

How was the month?

I got freed from school and went back home! But well, it's more like out of the frying pan and into the fire. I just need to survive the next two months, and then my mom and I will go to Hubei to pick up my dad from the metal-scrubbing competition and we'll all go to Xinjiang again! We toured around the north last year; this year we'll go to the south! To Qeshqer and stuff! (Qeshqer (قەشقەر) is more commonly spelled as "Kashgar," but since actually learning Uyghurche, it became increasingly silly because what do you mean, the two Q's becoming one K and one G? Maybe it has something to do with the pronunciation?)

If you were in my Discord server, you'd probably have heard me going on a rant about how I wanted a 4.0 GPA but knew I couldn't get it but I still want it anyways even though I knew I didn't need it and couldn't get it but I wanted it anyways and I think I could — you get the idea. Well, guess what I ended up getting? But the crazy thing was, I was certain I would do badly on a course. I threw away 5% on an assignment I knew I couldn't do so I just gave up on it, so I was capped at 95%, and that's not counting the points I'd already lost. I was preparing for a B-. But I ended up getting an A+?? When the cutoff was 97%? Either I got the weighting completely wrong or the professor changed the weighting to curve people. Either way, nice!

(June 12th update: Apparently my professor changed my grades like, days after the submission deadline and lowered it to an A… My GPA is still at 4 though so it doesn't matter.)

The reverse engineering course was also really fun; I'm glad I took it. I was quite worried at first, you see, because it used to have a prerequisite that later got removed but the course content did not change. Good that it got removed, because that prerequisite course was limited to people in the computer science program, in which I am not. But I was worried that I would do badly. But it turned out well! All of them turned out well, however, I knew that even if I time-traveled back to panicking past me, I would not have convinced myself to stop panicking. Such is the way things are.

Last weekend I went to the XDF near my home trying to scam them off a practice exam, because back when I consulted them about TOEFL classes they would give me a practice exam and then decide which class to recommend me. So I wanted to ask them about GRE classes and see if I could just take a practice exam, see my scores, and just leave. Turned out they didn't offer GRE practice exams, because they only had one level of classes, unlike TOEFL where there were multiple levels. Damn; I was so down for scamming them. But well.
